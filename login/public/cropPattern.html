Crop Pattern

Crop Pattern 1: Kharif

It is a rainy season crop grown from July to October. e.g. Paddy, barley, soyabean, pigeon pea, maize, cotton, groundnut, sugar beat, urad, moong, lobia, millets, til, jute, toor, sugar cane, lady finger (Okara), green gram and black gram, etc.

See More Info

Crop Pattern 2: Rabi

It is a winter season crop grown from November to April. e.g. Wheat, gram, peas, mustard, linseed, berseem, lentil, potatoes, tobacco, etc.

See More Info

Crop Pattern 3: Zaid

It is a crop which is planted mainly during the summer season from April to June or planted in a different season, in accordance with specific crops. e.g. Cucumber, pumpkin, water-melon, red-melon, gourd, ‘torai’, green chillis, tomatoes, and sunflower, etc.
